Alternative for physical study planner

Why this app was made

I wanted to make a study planner that is useful to both students and self-learners.

Like most students, I have to study a lot of materials for exams. The problem is, many exams have no content scope. This means you have to study everything the course provided. This can be very stressful for mainly two reasons. First, you feel anxious because you are not sure if your studying progress is on track. Second, you don't know that you are behind and you discover that too late. This anxiety double itself when you are graded 100% on the final exam, or if you have to take a professional exam that could be a once-in-a-lifetime chance.

This app focuses on creating a study plan for each material and simply forecast what's the progress will be at the end. The forecast feature has two main benefits. The first one is that you are motivated because you can visualize that every small progress count. Another one is that if you are behind schedule, you see it right away before it's too late.

As for self-learners, this app provides both flexibility and scope to study. When you want to learn something you're passionate about, you could feel like there are too many materials available on this planet. This app provide a structure to put you on track. Supported types of plans are: tracking amount, tracking chapters (book or video), and tracking comprehension percentage.
